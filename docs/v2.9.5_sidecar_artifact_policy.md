# v2.9.5 Sidecar Artifact Policy

Date: 2026-04-06
Status: Active sidecar policy for RC revisions

This document fixes how additive sidecars are treated on the `v2.9.5` line.
The policy is intentionally conservative:

- sidecars are allowed as additive outputs
- sidecars do not redefine the frozen manifest / inventory schema
- sidecars are represented in inventory via `generated_outputs`, not via new top-level inventory keys
- sidecars are not promoted into `required_outputs_by_mode` unless a future version explicitly freezes that change

## Inventory interpretation

For `v2.9.5`, `output_inventory.json` should be read as a known artifact catalog plus completion basis,
not as a full sidecar contract registry.

This means:

- sidecars may appear in `generated_outputs`
- sidecars do not become completion blockers by default
- replay and validators may read sidecars when present
- replay and validators must state explicitly when a missing sidecar is only observational versus contract-relevant

The code-level source for this catalog is `crisp/v29/sidecar_policy.py`.

## Interpretation notes

`theta_rule1_resolution.json` is the one exception that has stronger replay meaning today.
Rule1-capable runs depend on it for replay-level traceability, so replay audit can mark the run `UNCLEAR`
when the sidecar is missing in a Rule1 run.

`rule3_trace_summary.json` remains observational. It improves operator explainability and replay context,
but it does not change verdicts, SCV reason codes, completion semantics, or Cap truth-source logic.
